## Summary

This amendment formally defines the scope, deliverables, and completion checklists for **Phase 9 (Meta-Loop Implementation)** and **Phase 10 (Fitness Vector Integration)**. These phases extend the Prolog Substrate's capabilities to include autonomous self-optimization and integrated telemetry reporting to the Root Spine.

---

## Phase 9: Meta-Loop Implementation

**Scope**: Implementing the autonomous self-modification engine for the Prolog substrate.
**Deliverables**:
- `agents/prolog-substrate/meta/improve.pl`: The Observe-Introspect-Construct-Evaluate-Propose loop.
- `agents/prolog-substrate/meta/introspect.pl`: Predicate analysis and performance measurement.
- `agents/prolog-substrate/tests/test_meta_loop.pl`: Verification of the improvement cycle.

**Completion Checklist**:
- [ ] `swipl -g "consult('agents/prolog-substrate/tests/test_meta_loop.pl'), run_tests, halt."` returns 0.
- [ ] `propose_improvement/2` establishes a stable identity mapping for the safety baseline.

---

## Phase 10: Fitness Vector Integration

**Scope**: Exporting substrate-level performance and health signals to the global Root Spine telemetry layer.
**Deliverables**:
- `agents/prolog-substrate/meta/fitness.pl`: The aggregate fitness scorer.
- `agents/prolog-substrate/core/otel_bridge.pl`: Metric batching and dispatch to the OTel collector.
- `agents/prolog-substrate/tests/test_fitness.pl`: Verification of fitness metric accuracy.

**Completion Checklist**:
- [ ] `swipl -g "consult('agents/prolog-substrate/tests/test_fitness.pl'), run_tests, halt."` returns 0.
- [ ] `sati_central.prolog.substrate_fitness_score` reflects dynamic latency and diversity measurements.

## Rationale

Formalizing these phases ensures compliance with §1 Anti-Slop Rule 8. It provides the **Crucible** with a canonical checklist to verify against, preventing the architectural drift observed during the initial Phase 10 implementation.
